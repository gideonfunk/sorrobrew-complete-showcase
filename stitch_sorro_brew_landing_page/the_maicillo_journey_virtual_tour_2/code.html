Rooted in Resilience

Before the coffee trees arrived, the Maicillo flourished. Known as the "grain of the sun," this hardy ancestral sorghum has anchored Salvadoran agriculture for centuries, thriving in volcanic soil where others might falter.

It was never just a crop; it was a communal pulse. From the 'atol de maicillo' shared at sunrise to the roasted seeds brewed by mountain families during lean harvests, it represents a lineage of self-sufficiency and deep respect for the land.

"Maicillo doesn't just grow; it waits. It endures the dry season to offer its gold when the rains return."

The Alchemy

The Slow Transformation

The secret lies in the heat. Unlike coffee beans which are often flash-roasted, Maicillo requires a patient, rhythmic toast. We use traditional clay ovens and open-air fires to caramelize the natural sugars within the grain.

This slow-roasting process creates the signature Sorro Brew profile: deep notes of toasted cacao, roasted walnut, and a subtle earthiness that lingers like a warm memory.
