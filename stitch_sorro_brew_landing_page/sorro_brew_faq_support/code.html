Commonly Asked

What exactly is Maicillo? expand_more
Maicillo is an ancient, heirloom sorghum grain native to the high-altitude regions of El Salvador. Unlike common commercial crops, it requires minimal water and thrives in volcanic soil, resulting in a nutrient-dense grain that, when roasted using our heritage methods, mimics the deep complexity of coffee.
How does the taste compare to traditional coffee? expand_more
Expect a full-bodied experience with a nutty, toasted profile. While it shares the dark, roasted notes of a medium-dark roast coffee, it carries a natural sweetness and a cleaner finish without the bitter aftertaste or acidity that often plagues lower-quality beans.
Is Sorro Brew truly caffeine-free? expand_more
Yes. Maicillo is naturally caffeine-free. We do not use chemical decaffeination processes because there is no caffeine to remove in the first place. This makes it the perfect ritual for evening winding down or for those with high caffeine sensitivity.
Where do you source your ingredients? expand_more
We work directly with small-scale ancestral farmers in the San Vicente region of El Salvador. By cutting out middle-men, we ensure that over 70% of the final product value stays within the local farming communities, preserving traditional agricultural practices.
How do I brew the perfect cup at home? expand_more
Sorro Brew is versatile. For the best results, use a French Press with 2 tablespoons per 8oz of water just off the boil (205°F). Let it steep for 5 minutes—slightly longer than coffee—to allow the heritage grains to fully release their toasted oils.
